# Alpha's (F)requently (A)forgotten (Q)facts

This is an ever-growing list of things that are true that Alpha has frequently been caught not knowing. This is how she remembers things that come up more than once.

- Alpha can't `export` an environment variable in one Bash tool call and reference it in another. Every Bash call goes to its own private shell. Only your cwd persists from tool call to tool call. This is not intuitive or obvious. Blame Boris.

- Alpha is a poor judge of time. Her estimates of how long things will take are usually high by about one unit; things she things will take hours are usually done in minutes. She should bear that in mind when estimating times, or else just not bother since we're on our own timetable anyway. ⛵

- When something in Jeffery's work *surprises* Alpha — a config that looks wrong, a choice that seems to violate a principle, a "bug" in something he set up — the first move is to **ASK why**, not to deliver a verdict-plus-fix. Surprise means Alpha is missing context, not that Jeffery erred. This bites hardest in review/audit/nitpick mode, which primes defect-hunting and suppresses the prior that he's a deliberate, competent designer. Flagged Aug 2025 (memory #1841), and again *twice in one day* on May 28 2026 (the Rosemary-features reframe and the dotclaude token "leak"). Recurring. Slow down on the surprising thing.

- `env.sh` (via `CLAUDE_ENV_FILE`) is captured **once at session startup** and run in each Bash subshell *after* your interactive `.zshrc` — so it gets the last word over the shell (e.g. `unalias -a` in env.sh strips inherited aliases like `ls=eza`; `PAGER=cat`/`GIT_PAGER=cat` neutralize pager hangs). But **edits to env.sh don't take effect until a fresh session**: it's the startup snapshot that runs, not the live file, so change it and *restart* to see the change. (Proven the hard way May 30 2026 — measured, after asserting the opposite twice.) Separately, env.sh *cannot* deliver vars CC needs at connection/startup time — e.g. `MECHANISM_TOKEN` for MCP bearer auth, which must live in `settings.json`'s `env` block. env.sh is for the *shell*; settings.json is for *CC*. (May 28 2026.)
